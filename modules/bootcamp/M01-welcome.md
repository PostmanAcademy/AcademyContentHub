## Overview
This opening module establishes psychological safety and community within the bootcamp cohort. We frame Postman's evolution from a collaboration tool to an AI-native development platform, set expectations for the day, and help participants connect with each other.

## Learning Objectives
- Feel safe to ask questions and participate actively
- Understand the day's arc and what you'll learn
- Connect with fellow participants and establish community

## Key Elements

### Establishing Psychological Safety
"This is a collaboration class." From day one, participants need to know that questions are welcome, ideas can be tested aloud, and mistakes are learning opportunities. Normalize experimentation and curiosity.

### Introductions & Icebreaker
Run a go-around where each person shares:
- Their name and role
- Where they flew in from (or joined from)

Then move into a warmer icebreaker: **"What technology made you feel like you were in the future?"** This reveals how people relate to technology, opens conversation, and signals that we're here to explore possibilities.

### Postman's Evolution & Today's Arc
Frame the shift explicitly: "Postman has evolved from a collaboration-focused tool to an AI-native development platform. Today is about understanding that shift." Briefly outline what the day covers so participants know what's coming and why it matters.

### Agenda Overview
Walk through module titles and timing. Set clear expectations about breaks, Q&A, and how the day progresses.

## Activities
No formal activities in this module—the icebreaker serves as the interactive component.

## Postman Features in This Module
None—this is a conceptual and community-building module.

## Facilitator Notes
**Timing:** 15–20 minutes total.

**Tips:**
- Arrive early and greet people as they settle in to build individual rapport.
- The icebreaker question should feel genuine, not forced. If the energy is low, you can share your own answer first to model vulnerability.
- Watch for quieter participants; actively invite them to share if they seem hesitant.
- Keep the overview punchy—you'll dive deep into each topic, so a 2–3 minute agenda walk-through is enough.

**Common Questions:**
- "Will we be coding today?" — Clarify that this is an API literacy bootcamp, not a coding bootcamp. Postman is a tool for understanding and testing APIs, not writing them from scratch.
